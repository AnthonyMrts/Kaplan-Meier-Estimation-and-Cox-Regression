# Kaplan-Meier Estimation and Cox Regression
In this project, classical survival methods were applied to a real-
world glioma dataset from the Chinese Glioma Genome Atlas
(CGGA). Overall survival was employed across World Health
Organization (WHO) grades II, III and IV, using Kaplan-Meier
curves to compare survival distributions and the Cox proportional
hazards model to quantify the effect of tumor grade, age and gender
on the hazard of death. The results shown a clear and statistically
significant degradation in survival with increasing WHO grade:
patients with WHO III tumors have a markedly higher risk of death
compared to those with WHO II, and WHO IV (glioblastoma)
patients exhibited the poorest prognosis. Increasing age was also
associated with worse survival, whereas gender did not have a
significant effect. These findings illustrate how standard survival
techniques can capture clinically meaningful patterns in
heterogeneous cancer data and provide a baseline for more
advanced data science models such as DeepSurv.
